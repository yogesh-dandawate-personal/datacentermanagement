## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

iNetZero's go-to-market strategy focuses on becoming the **standard operating system for net-zero data centers**. We'll compete on:
- **Specialization** (data center focus, not generalists)
- **Speed** (4-6 week implementation vs. 16-20 weeks)
- **Value** (50% cheaper with proven ROI)
- **Intelligence** (AI agents, not just reports)

**3-Year Goal**: Establish market leadership with 120+ customers and $14M+ ARR by end of 2028

---

## MARKET SEGMENTATION & TARGET PERSONAS

### Primary Target: Enterprise Data Center Operators

**Segment**: Companies with 2-5 owned/leased data centers
- **Market size**: ~600 companies globally
- **Annual energy spend**: $500K-$5M
- **ESG budget**: $100K-$500K/year
- **Decision cycle**: 8-12 weeks
- **Deal size**: $50K-$150K/year

**Target Personas**:

1. **VP Operations** (Primary buyer)
   - Pain: Energy costs increasing, facility underutilized
   - Goal: Optimize operations, reduce costs
   - Motivation: Bottom-line impact, bonus tied to efficiency

2. **Chief Sustainability Officer** (Economic buyer)
   - Pain: Investor scrutiny on ESG metrics, compliance gaps
   - Goal: Meet net-zero targets, prove progress
   - Motivation: Board credibility, investor confidence

3. **CFO / Finance** (Approval authority)
   - Pain: Energy audit costs high, ESG uncertainty
   - Goal: Cost containment, risk mitigation
   - Motivation: Expense management, regulatory compliance

---

### Secondary Target: Colocation Operators

**Segment**: Companies operating 5-20+ leased data centers
- **Market size**: ~200 companies globally (Equinix, Digital Realty, CoreWeave, etc.)
- **Annual energy spend**: $5M-$50M+
- **ESG budget**: $500K-$5M/year
- **Decision cycle**: 12-16 weeks
- **Deal size**: $150K-$300K+/year

**Unique Needs**:
- Multi-facility reporting and benchmarking
- Customer SLA compliance (PUE targets, uptime)
- Regulatory reporting (SEC, investor disclosures)

---

### Tertiary Target: Hyperscalers & Cloud Providers

**Segment**: AWS, Google Cloud, Microsoft, Meta, Apple
- **Market size**: ~10 companies (but very high value)
- **Annual energy spend**: $500M-$5B+
- **ESG budget**: $10M-$100M+/year
- **Decision cycle**: 16-24 weeks
- **Deal size**: Custom ($500K-$5M+/year)

**Unique Needs**:
- Extreme scale (100+ facilities globally)
- Custom integrations with internal systems
- White-label capability
- Real-time optimization at scale

**What they evaluate**:

- How does iNetZero compare to alternatives?

**Our Activities**:

### Stage 3: Evaluation (Weeks 7-12)

**What they need**:
- Proof of concept / trial
- Integration assessment
- Security review
- Pricing discussion
- Internal stakeholder alignment

- ✅ Customer success manager assignment

## CUSTOMER SUCCESS & RETENTION STRATEGY

### Onboarding & Implementation (First 30 days)

**Customer Success Manager** (1 CSM per 20 customers):
- Kickoff call (week 1): Confirm scope, timeline, success metrics
- Weekly check-ins (weeks 1-4): Monitor progress, identify blockers
- Training session (week 3): Administrator training, user onboarding
- Go-live support (week 4): 24/7 support for launch

**Success Metrics**:
- 100% on-time implementation
- 90% on-time data ingestion
- 80% on-time dashboard population

### Ongoing Success (Months 2-12)

**Quarterly Business Reviews** (for Pro/Enterprise):
- Review results (energy savings, reporting progress)
- Identify new use cases and expansion opportunities
- Discuss roadmap and feedback

**Usage Monitoring**:
- Track engagement metrics (logins, feature usage)
- Proactive outreach if engagement drops
- Identify expansion opportunities

**Product Adoption**:
- Ensure all features are being used (agents, copilot, etc.)
- Advanced training for new features
- Certification program (optional)

### Retention & Expansion

**Retention Targets**:
- Year 1: 95% retention (early customer losses acceptable)
- Year 2: 97% retention
- Year 3+: 98%+ retention

**Expansion Levers**:
- Add facilities (Starter → Pro, Pro → Enterprise)
- Upgrade tiers (more features, support)
- Professional services (custom integrations)
- Premium support and SLAs

**Net Retention Rate Target**: 130%+ (via expansion)

### Churn Prevention

**Early Warning Indicators**:
- No logins for 2+ weeks
- Support tickets with concerns
- Executive changes (key stakeholder leaves)
- Mergers/acquisitions

**Intervention Strategy**:
- CSM proactive outreach
- Executive business review
- Product roadmap customization
- Special pricing/incentives if appropriate

## COMPETITIVE WIN/LOSS STRATEGY

### Win/Loss Program

**Methodology**:
- Conduct 1-2 interviews/month with lost deals
- Analyze competitive response (which competitor won, why)
- Share learnings with sales & product

**Key Questions**:
- Why did you choose [competitor]?
- What were evaluation criteria?
- What would have won this deal?
- How can we improve?

**Adjustments Based on Findings**:
- Product roadmap refinements
- Messaging updates
- Sales strategy changes
- Competitive response tactics

### Battle Cards

**Competitive Messaging by Competitor**:

**vs. Workiva**:
- "We're 50% cheaper, 3x faster to implement, and specialize in data centers"
- "Workiva is great for multi-industry ESG, we're built specifically for DCs"

**vs. Nlyte**:
- "We add emissions + compliance + optimization to your DCIM"
- "Complementary, not competitive—together you get complete DC management"

**vs. Vertiv**:
- "We handle the ESG compliance you're missing, you handle the cooling optimization"
- "Partnership > competition"
